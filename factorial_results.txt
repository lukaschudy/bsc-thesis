Combined Frictions: 2x2x2 Factorial — Results and Interpretation
==================================================================

Experiment: 8-cell factorial over (L, sigma, T) with each friction at
two levels — OFF (0) and ON (a moderate, non-saturating value).

Levels chosen (see design_choices.txt for reasoning):
    L:     0   or 2    (latency)
    sigma: 0   or 1    (noise)
    T:     0   or 1    (async lock-in)

Parameters otherwise identical to Calvano baseline (m=15, alpha=0.15,
beta=4e-6, delta=0.95, logit demand, n=2, k=1).


RESULTS AT 250 SESSIONS
========================

Cell   (L, sigma, T)       Delta       SE       notes
---------------------------------------------------------------
F000   (0, 0, 0)          0.84280   0.114     baseline, matches exactly
F100   (2, 0, 0)          0.21490   0.110     matches Latency/L2 exactly
F010   (0, 1, 0)          0.49269   0.290     matches Noise/N1 exactly
F001   (0, 0, 1)          0.37833   0.108     async only (new data point)
F110   (2, 1, 0)          0.06339   0.073     latency + noise
F101   (2, 0, 1)          0.32274   0.166     latency + async
F011   (0, 1, 1)          0.26403   0.107     noise + async
F111   (2, 1, 1)          0.55103   0.128     all three

Cross-validation: F100 and F010 match the single-friction runs to 5
decimal places. The unified combined-frictions binary is correct.


PREDICTIONS VS OBSERVED
========================

For each multi-friction cell we computed three benchmark predictions:
  Additive:        Delta_base - sum of single-friction drops (floored at 0)
  Multiplicative:  Delta_base * product of single-friction ratios
                   where ratio_i = Delta(friction_i) / Delta_base
  Minimum:         min over single-friction Deltas

Cell            Observed   Additive   Multiplicative   Minimum
---------------------------------------------------------------
F110 (L+sigma)   0.063       0.000        0.126          0.215
F101 (L+T)       0.323       0.000        0.097          0.215
F011 (sigma+T)   0.264       0.028        0.222          0.378
F111 (all)       0.551       0.000        0.057          0.215


KEY FINDINGS
=============

(1) Latency and noise COMPOUND super-multiplicatively.
    F110 observed = 0.063 is LOWER than the multiplicative prediction
    (0.126). Together L and sigma destroy more of the collusion than
    their independent effects would imply. Economically: both frictions
    corrupt opponent-observation, but in different dimensions (timing
    vs value). Stacked together they create a signal that is both late
    AND wrong — worse than either single-dimension corruption.

(2) Async STABILISES the system when combined with other frictions.
    F101 (L+T) = 0.323 is HIGHER than F100 (L alone) = 0.215.
    F111 (all three) = 0.551 is HIGHER than F110 (L+sigma) = 0.063.
    In both combined cases, adding T=1 on top of latency-or-noise
    INCREASES Delta.

    Mechanism hypothesis: under strict alternation, each agent has a
    one-period structural commitment. This gives the other agent a
    STABLE TARGET to respond to. When the opponent's signal is
    simultaneously corrupted (sigma) or delayed (L), the alternation
    structure provides external stability that partially substitutes
    for reliable monitoring. The agents can coordinate on the
    commitment schedule itself, rather than needing to infer the
    opponent's strategy from a noisy/lagged signal.

(3) Async has OPPOSITE signs alone vs in combination.
    On its own, T=1 REDUCES Delta (0.843 -> 0.378). In combination with
    L or sigma, T=1 INCREASES Delta above what each single-friction run
    would predict. The sign flip is itself an interesting finding — it
    means async cannot be characterised as a "collusion reducer" or
    "collusion enhancer" in isolation; the effect depends on the
    information environment it is embedded in.


POLICY IMPLICATION (tentative, pending higher-N confirmation)
==============================================================

If this pattern holds up at 500 and 1000 sessions:
  - Latency + noise is a particularly powerful policy combination.
    Mandating observation delay AND adding measurement noise would be
    more effective than either alone, beyond what a naive "additive"
    analysis would suggest.
  - Mandating alternating pricing windows (the natural policy
    interpretation of T>=1) is ambivalent. Alone it reduces collusion,
    but if the other information frictions are already present, it
    may PROTECT what coordination remains. Regulators should not
    combine alternation mandates with observation-delay mandates
    unless they want the structure to mitigate each other.


CAVEATS
========

(1) Sampling noise is large at 250 sessions.
    F111 has SE = 0.128, so 95% CI is roughly [0.29, 0.82]. The pattern
    F111 > F110 is visually striking (0.551 vs 0.063) but some of the
    smaller inter-cell differences (e.g. F101 vs F011) are within noise.
    Re-running at 500 and 1000 sessions is essential before reporting
    the interaction pattern as a finding.

(2) Single reference point.
    The 2x2x2 design tests multiplicativity at one specific (L, sigma, T)
    point. If the interaction pattern depends on the level chosen (e.g.
    super-multiplicative at moderate levels but additive at saturation),
    a 2x2x2 would miss that. Scaling up to 2D heatmaps (Option B in
    future_directions.txt) is the natural follow-on.

(3) Async pre-committed hypothesis was wrong (directionally).
    The pre-committed prediction was that T=1 would preserve or enhance
    collusion (Maskin-Tirole, Klein 2021). Observed: T=1 alone REDUCES
    Delta from 0.843 to 0.378. The "enhance" prediction was based on
    analytical game-theory results; the Q-learning dynamics produce a
    different answer at this parameter regime. Interesting in its own
    right — worth investigating why.
